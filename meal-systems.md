# Meal Systems

A set of practical systems for feeding a family consistently without relying on willpower, constant decision-making, or daily reinvention.

The goal is not perfect nutrition.  
The goal is a system that works on tired weekdays.

---

## Core Principles

- Reduce daily decisions  
- Plan at the system level, not the meal level  
- Accept repetition  
- Optimize for energy, not variety  
- Build around real constraints (time, kids, work)

---

## Weekly Meal Structure

Instead of deciding meals from scratch, assign categories to days:

- Monday → Simple protein + vegetable + carb
- Tuesday → Pasta / grains
- Wednesday → Leftovers or freezer meal
- Thursday → Sheet pan / one-pot meal
- Friday → Flexible / takeout
- Weekend → Higher effort or social meals

This reduces decision fatigue while maintaining flexibility.

---

## Component-Based Cooking

Cook in components rather than full meals:

- Protein: chicken, ground meat, beans
- Carbohydrates: rice, pasta, potatoes
- Vegetables: roasted, steamed, sautéed

Meals become combinations:
- chicken + rice + carrots
- beans + tortillas + avocado

This allows:
- faster assembly
- easier adaptation for kids
- less waste

---

## Default Meal Rotation

Maintain a small set of “default meals”:

- 5–10 dinners that repeat regularly
- 2–3 breakfasts on rotation
- 2–3 lunches on rotation

Examples:
- pasta + vegetables
- shredded chicken + rice bowls
- eggs + toast + fruit

The goal is familiarity, not novelty. 
"Default" meals can also be delivery 
from favorite restaurants, grocery store 
premade meals, or meal plan delivery systems. 

---

## Freezer Buffer System

Always maintain 2–3 backup meals:

- frozen soup
- pre-cooked chicken
- store-bought emergency meals

This prevents:
- last-minute stress
- reliance on takeout when energy is low

---

## Toddler Adaptation Layer

Instead of separate meals for children:

- modify texture (cut, mash, soften)
- deconstruct meals (serve components separately)
- include one “safe” food

This reduces:
- extra cooking
- mealtime conflict

---

## Grocery System

Use a repeatable structure:

- Proteins (2–3 options)
- Carbs (2–3 options)
- Vegetables (3–5 options)
- Fruits
- Snacks
- Backup/freezer items

Avoid planning exact meals at the grocery stage.

---

## Time-Based Cooking Strategy

Match meals to energy levels:

- Low energy → pre-cooked or assembly meals
- Medium energy → simple stovetop meals
- High energy → batch cooking or new recipes

Not every day should require effort.

## Notes

A good meal system:
- works on busy weekdays
- reduces cognitive load
- supports both adults and children
- is repeatable without burnout

It should feel boring in the best possible way.
